Chop Suey

Chop suey is fast and easy to make. My mother always made this recipe; we love it as-is, but you can substitute a can of Chinese mixed vegetables for the bean sprouts if you want more color and texture. Serve over hot rice and top with chow mein noodles and soy sauce.

  • Prep Time: 20 mins
  • Cook Time: 20 mins
  • Total Time: 40 mins
  • Servings: 4

Chop Suey - Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup shortening
  • 1 1/2 cups diced pork loin
  • 1 cup diced onion
  • 1 cup diced celery
  • 1 cup hot water
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 (14.5 ounce) can beans sprouts, drained and rinsed
  • 1/3 cold water
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 2 teaspoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon white sugar

How to Make

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients
  2. Heat shortening in a large, deep skillet over medium-high meat. Add pork and sear until it turns white.
  3. Add onion and saut√© for 5 minutes. Stir in celery, hot water, salt, and pepper. Cover skillet and simmer for 5 minutes.
  4. Add sprouts and bring mixture to a boil.
  5. Combine cold water, cornstarch, soy sauce, and sugar in a small bowl.
  6. Add to skillet mixture and cook until thickened to desired consistency, about 5 minutes.
